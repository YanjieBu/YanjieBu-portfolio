Project: Brisbane Wildtrack

"Brisbane Wildtrack" is an enjoyable app that blends wildlife with outdoor activities.

The design concept of this Brisbane wildlife app integrates learning, science popularization, entertainment, social interaction, and courses into a multifunctional platform. Users can not only access comprehensive information about Australian wildlife but also gain deeper insights into and protection of these species through interactive experiences and practical activities. The highlight of the app is to combine outdoor activities with wildlife knowledge, rewarding users by completing outdoor challenges to increase engagement and experience.

In our team, Nature Navigator, which consists of five members, my primary responsibility was in the design area. I took part in the preliminary research phase, gathering and analyzing user needs, and subsequently developed detailed user personas to guide our design process. I independently designed the app icon and the team logo and created the web interface prototypes to ensure a clear and intuitive user experience. Additionally, I contributed to the development of the homepage and Wiki page, establishing a solid foundation for the project's overall visual identity and user experience. Through my design efforts, our team's project achieved enhanced visual appeal and usability.

Design Evolution

Establishment of the Project Theme

Week4 & Week5

In Week4 & Week5, our team, Nature Navigator, finalized the project theme, focusing on animal conservation, particularly displaying wildlife information for Australia or Brisbane area. As the lead designer, I sketched the initial interface on a whiteboard and collaborated with teammates to discuss the website's functionality and layout, incorporating elements from my previous poster design. Together, we established the basic project framework and gathered relevant datasets, including information on koala populations, storm tide data, and other local wildlife data. We also engaged in cross-group discussions, sharing our project concept and receiving feedback from other groups. This process helped us identify design flaws and inspired new ideas.

My poster played a lot of roles and gave our team some inspiration. Some of the functions adopted my previous poster.

Preliminary Research and Design of the Project

Week5 & Week6 & Week7

During Weeks 5&6&7, I designed our team logo (see left image), featuring a friendly koala as the main visual element. The koala is set against green foliage, symbolizing the natural environment and conveying a sense of warmth and approachability. This design highlights our commitment to wildlife conservation and resonates with our focus on Australia’s native animals. The simplicity of the logo makes it versatile, allowing it to be effectively used on our website and promotional materials while clearly reflecting our team's values.

Additionally, I designed the app icon (see right image), combining a compass and a kangaroo to symbolize exploration and guidance. The compass represents our mission to help users navigate the world of wildlife conservation, while the kangaroo signifies the unique wildlife of Australia. Surrounding the compass are animal footprints, reinforcing the theme of ecological preservation and our dedication to leaving a positive mark on the environment. This app icon is easily recognizable and aligns with the broader theme of our project.

In the following stages, I conducted a comprehensive user research process, combining offline surveys with online interviews. Leveraging AI tools, I facilitated user interviews to gather detailed insights. This research allowed me to accurately capture the needs, motivations, and pain points of potential users. Based on these findings, I created three user personas, each representing a distinct type of user for our app: the nature lover, the animal protector, and the outdoor enthusiast. These personas helped to clarify the website's target audience and guided the design decisions to align with users' specific expectations and requirements.

Building on the initial hand-drawn prototypes created both in and outside of class, I developed a comprehensive series of web prototypes using Figma. These sketches served as the foundation, allowing me to translate the rough concepts into a more refined and interactive digital design. Through Figma, I was able to enhance the flow, add detailed UI elements, and visually represent the website's intended user experience. This approach helped to clarify the functionality and interactions within the platform, ensuring each component aligns with the needs identified during user research.

I developed a series of web prototypes using Figma to refine our app's interface and functionality. The design flow includes multiple pages: an introduction screen, login page, homepage with a map interface, personal profile, and an activity page. Each page integrates elements essential for user engagement, such as a map to track wildlife locations, challenges to encourage exploration, and an activity review for social interaction.

The homepage allows users to locate and explore nearby animals with an interactive map, complete with icons indicating various wildlife types. A search bar is available for users to filter animals or locations by name, enhancing accessibility. The personal profile page provides users with a space to track their activities, connect with friends, and view completed challenges. Additionally, the activity page showcases wildlife-related events, rewards, and a product section, all designed to support an immersive wildlife exploration experience.

The search functionality within the prototype is designed to allow users to explore wildlife in specific locations with ease. Starting from the main search results page, users can view multiple animal icons spread across a map, each icon representing wildlife spotted in different areas. Users can zoom into a selected region to find more detailed results on the “Search Result Zoom” page. Here, they gain insights into specific locations, including user posts and sightings. Finally, the "Animal Information" page provides comprehensive details on the chosen animal, including habitat, behavior, and legal protection information. This page integrates user-generated posts, offering a community-driven perspective and allowing users to share experiences and sightings, enhancing engagement and knowledge-sharing among wildlife enthusiasts.

Establishment of Project MVP

Week7 & Week8

During our group project on animal conservation, we created a paper prototype to visualize the design and functionality of our web interface. Our goal was to craft a user-friendly platform highlighting wildlife conservation activities around Brisbane. I was responsible for sketching on the whiteboard and took part in role-based testing sessions. Our prototype covered the welcome page, navigation bar, profile settings, social features, and interactive content displays. This process revealed important design insights—specifically, we identified that certain interaction buttons were overly complex, and the activity page was too information-dense, causing potential user confusion. Based on these observations, we refined our design to prioritize simplicity and clarity, ensuring a more intuitive user experience. This experience underscored the value of iterative design, user feedback, and effective team collaboration in enhancing the functionality and accessibility of our interface.

Project Web Programming

Week9 & Week10

During the development phase of our website, our team began programming the web pages, and I contributed to the HTML and CSS coding for both the homepage and the wiki page. This hands-on experience allowed me to deepen my understanding of front-end development and apply design principles to ensure a cohesive and visually appealing interface. Additionally, I took the opportunity to learn from my teammates, acquiring valuable insights and skills in JavaScript and PHP programming. This cross-functional learning broadened my technical skill set and enabled me to contribute more effectively to our collaborative project.

Currently, I am working on the layout design for the homepage and wiki page of our project to ensure they are functional and visually organized. The homepage features an interactive map with markers representing various wildlife locations around Brisbane, allowing users to search for specific animals and explore their habitats. The wiki page displays a series of cards with images, scientific names, and conservation statuses for different species, providing users with a quick overview and a search bar for easy navigation. As we continue developing more features, I plan to refine these layouts further, focusing on both aesthetics and usability to make the interface more engaging and intuitive for users.

Moving forward, I will be refining the prototypes to create high-fidelity versions that will be implemented in VSCode and integrated into the website. By enhancing the design's detail and realism, I aim to provide a clear vision for the final interface. Additionally, with the Java and PHP skills I've been acquiring, I'm now equipped to contribute more directly to the project's functionality. This knowledge will allow me to add and modify features independently, enhancing the interactivity and user experience of our web application.

Reflection & Journals

Project Planning and Team Collaboration

At the beginning, our diverse team struggled to align our different perspectives, given our varied backgrounds. Establishing clear roles, regular communication, and setting a shared vision became essential to keep us on track. Research emphasizes that inclusivity and regular communication improve cohesion in diverse teams (Meyer et al., 2014). This experience taught me how crucial it is to foster cohesion and align everyone with the project goals, a skill that is essential for future collaborations (Dyer et al., 2013).

Prototyping and Iterative Design

Developing the prototype exposed usability issues, such as overly complex buttons and excessive information on certain pages. User feedback was invaluable for simplifying the design, aligning with iterative design models where improvements come through repetitive testing and refinement (Nielsen & Molich, 1990). This process underscored the importance of clarity in user interactions and highlighted how iterative feedback is key to creating a user-friendly product.

Feature Prioritization and Project Scope

After consulting with our tutor, our team decided to focus on core features and streamline the project scope. Initially, we aimed to include a wide range of functionalities, but this ambition led to technical challenges and time constraints, stressing the team and impacting progress. This experience highlighted the need to balance ambition with practicality, as Brooks (1995) notes in The Mythical Man-Month. By narrowing our scope, we were able to focus on delivering key features that best served user needs, reinforcing the importance of prioritizing quality over quantity to achieve a successful outcome.

Presentation Skills and Time Management

One of the biggest lessons I learned was the importance of rehearsing and managing time during presentations. We ran out of time while presenting and had to rush through some sections, which affected the impact of our work. This feedback underscored the importance of practice and pacing to ensure that every team member can effectively communicate their part. I now understand that a well-paced presentation is just as crucial as the content itself.

Technical Development and Dataset Challenges

Integrating external datasets turned out to be more challenging than anticipated, as we struggled to find compatible data sources. This experience taught me the importance of early research and preparation, especially when relying on external data. I also became more comfortable with API integration and better understood how to manage the technical requirements for future projects. This learning process helped me develop resilience in troubleshooting and adapting when technical roadblocks arise.

Continuous Learning and Adaptation

I took every opportunity to expand my skills in web development, including JavaScript and PHP, by working closely with my teammates. I also gained insights into user-centered design, which has deepened my understanding of how to create effective, intuitive interfaces. Through this process, I learned to approach challenges as learning experiences and grew more confident in my technical and design abilities.

Journals and Growth

Writing weekly journals helped me cultivate a habit of self-assessment and allowed me to keep track of my progress, challenges, and learnings throughout the project. Each journal entry prompted me to evaluate what went well, what could have been improved, and how I could approach similar challenges in the future. This journaling process allowed me to recognize my strengths and weaknesses and adapt my approach accordingly. It reinforced the importance of continuous learning, personal growth, and adaptation. I came to understand that journaling is a critical part of professional development, as it enables me to make informed adjustments to my work and grow as a designer and collaborator.

Portfolio

In designing my portfolio website, I aimed to create a vibrant, interactive experience that would showcase my design skills while aligning with the theme of wildlife conservation. The website's layout is dynamic, featuring a moving sidebar that adapts as the user scrolls, making navigation seamless and user-friendly. One of the key highlights is the animated navbar, which changes according to the section displayed, adding an engaging layer to the browsing experience.

To maintain a cohesive aesthetic, I designed a custom background filled with whimsical animal illustrations, creating an immersive and relevant backdrop that reflects the site's focus. The vibrant colors and animal motifs are not only visually appealing but also serve to enhance the theme, tying the design together.

Throughout the development process, I used HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to implement the interactive elements. I focused on keeping the site responsive, ensuring accessibility across different devices. This project allowed me to combine technical skills with creative design, resulting in a portfolio that truly reflects my journey and passion for interactive design.
